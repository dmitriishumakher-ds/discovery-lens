Usability test session — Revolut
Task: Set up a new savings vault for "holiday fund" with monthly contributions
Participant: P11 — Female, 28, junior developer, Revolut user for ~2 years, never used vaults before
Observer: Sarah (UX research)
Date: Oct 18, 2025
Duration: ~18 min
Setup: Phone on stand, screen recording, think-aloud protocol

---

00:00 - Brief: "Set up a savings vault called 'Holiday Fund' with a goal of £1500. Set up an automatic monthly contribution of £100 from your main account."

00:25 - P opens Revolut. Stares at home screen briefly.
       Quote: "Vault. Okay. Where's vaults..."
       (P has used app for 2 years but never opened this feature.)

00:50 - P scrolls through tabs. Doesn't immediately see Vaults or Savings.
       Taps "Hub" tab. Sees options for Stocks, Crypto, Lifestyle, Savings.
       Quote: "Savings, that's it I think."

01:25 - Savings screen. Two options: "Savings account" and "Pockets."
       Long pause. P reads both descriptions.
       Quote: "Pockets... is that the same as a vault? Or is that different?"
       (Observer note: terminology confusion — "vault" is what the brief said, but the app calls them "Pockets" now.)

02:10 - P taps Pockets. Empty state with "Create your first Pocket" button.
       Quote: "Okay, so vault and pocket are the same thing then."
       (P checks Help by tapping "?" - confirms Pockets replaced Vaults in 2024 rename.)

02:40 - P taps "Create Pocket." Naming screen.
       Types "Holiday Fund." Picks an emoji (palm tree).
       Continue. Goal screen.

03:15 - Goal screen. P enters £1500. Selects target date — picks date 12 months from now.
       Quote: "If I do 12 months and want £1500, that's £125 a month. But the brief said £100. So... I'll just do £100 and the goal will take longer."
       (P does mental math, decides priorities.)

04:00 - Continue. Auto-transfer setup. Two options: "Round-ups" or "Recurring transfer."
       Quote: "I want recurring."
       Taps it. Asked: amount, frequency, source account.

04:40 - Fills in £100, monthly, on the 1st, from main GBP account.
       Quote: "Easy, that's clear."

05:15 - Confirmation screen. P reads through.
       Quote: "So... when does the first transfer happen? Today is the 18th. Does it transfer £100 right now, or wait until the 1st of next month?"
       (Confirmation screen says "Next contribution: Nov 1." But P had to look carefully.)

05:48 - P taps "Confirm." Pocket created.
       Lands on Pocket detail screen. Shows £0 balance, goal £1500, "Next: Nov 1, £100."

06:25 - P taps around the Pocket. Finds: "Add money," "Transfer out," "Edit goal," "Pause auto-transfer."
       Quote: "Wait can I transfer out? Like, what if I need the money? Is it locked?"
       Taps "Transfer out." Sees: "Transfer back to main account. Funds available immediately."
       Quote: "Oh good, it's not locked. I thought it might be."

07:30 - P notices interest rate at top of Pocket: "AER: 2.05%."
       Quote: "Two percent. Mm. Is that good? Probably not great compared to a real savings account. But it's better than zero."
       (P doesn't pursue this further but mentioned it as a passive observation.)

08:10 - P attempts to add a one-off £20 to test it.
       Taps "Add money." Enters £20. Confirms.
       Pocket now shows £20 balance.
       Quote: "Cool, that worked."

09:00 - Task complete. P expresses satisfaction overall.

---

OBSERVER NOTES:

Friction points (ranked by severity):
1. MEDIUM: "Vault" vs "Pocket" terminology mismatch. The product was renamed but external references (including this brief) still use "Vault." Caused 90 seconds of confusion at task start.
2. MEDIUM: Auto-transfer first contribution timing is shown but not prominent. P had to actively look for it.
3. MEDIUM: Interest rate (2.05% AER) shown but not contextualised. P questioned whether it's competitive without an answer.
4. LOW: "Hub" tab as the entry point for Savings — not the most discoverable label. Took ~50 seconds to find.

Positive moments:
- Goal-setting flow is clean and intuitive.
- Round-ups vs recurring transfer choice is clearly differentiated.
- Lock/unlock model (funds available immediately) was a quiet delighter — user expected funds might be locked.
- Emoji + name personalisation made the experience feel friendly.

Quote of the session:
"Pockets, vaults, whatever. The thing itself works. Just confused me for a minute."

Recommendations to discuss with team:
- Investigate cost of full migration of "Vault" → "Pocket" in marketing/onboarding/help articles
- Make first-contribution date/timing more prominent on confirmation screen
- Consider showing AER comparison or context ("X% above market average")
- Evaluate whether "Hub" is the right label for Savings entry point
- Lock/unlock model is a strength — consider making it more prominent in marketing
